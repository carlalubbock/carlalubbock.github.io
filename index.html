What is Cryptozoology?

Cryptozoology is the study of legendary creatures of myth and folklore whose existence are often disputed. Ivan T. Sanderson coined the term in around 1959, who published books surrounding cryptids alongside the works of Bernard Heuvelmans.
Cryptozoology covers a range of different monsters, from red-eyed beasts to hairy humanoids that can be found all over the planet, as there is no solid classification as to what can and cannot be a cryptid. A cryptid is recognised if it is present in folklore, rumours, or eyewitness accounts, and has animalistic or unexplainable qualities to it.
