Jackalope

Lepus antilocapra

The jackalope is a critter of the forest; a hare, or more specifically a jackrabbit, with antlers or horns of some kind.

Variations of the jackalope are found all around the world, records of which have been found from 13th century Persia and medieval and renaissance-period Europe, as well as legends from across Asia, the Americas, and Africa

Story/Context

They are said to be dangerous creatures, able to mimic human voice and other sounds. They are incredibly elusive, but one of the ways to catch a jackalope is to entice it with whiskey, the jackalope's beverage of choice. There is no one origin of the jackalope, but its name comes from the western USA as a combination of 'jackrabbit' and 'antelope'. Dispite the name, they usually sport antlers resembling a deer.
Some suspected jackalopes were hares suffering from shope papilloma virus, and many other instances were frankensteined taxidermies, though this creature has been truly sighted on occasion.
