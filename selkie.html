Selkie

No scientific name

The selkie is a creature of the sea; a beautiful species of shapeshifter that can switch between a seal and human form by taking off and putting on their seal pelt, much like a cloak. Their appearance can differ between folk, though they are largely depicted as female and larger than a regular grey seal.

This cryptid is found in Scotland and surrounding areas. They dwell in the ocean while in their seal forms, and are trapped on land in their human form

Story/Context

There are many stories surrounding the selkie, as they have been a muse of sorts for centuries, however a large portion of these recounts involve the selkie being lured onto land by a sailor or similar man and forced to marry him after he takes her pelt. She then must live out her days tethered to the land, longing for the sea, until she finds the cloak locked away somewhere and returns to her home. If the selkie has children in that time on land, they are usually left behind as the selkie flees, and it is often that child who discovers the sealskin.
The selkie is said to either shapeshift at will or only on particular nights, such as full moons.
