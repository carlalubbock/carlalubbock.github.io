Loch Ness Monster

Nessiteras rhombopteryx

Nessie, formally known as the Loch Ness Monster, is a monster of the lake; a large marine being with a long neck, possibly resembling a type of sea serpent, dragon, or prehistoric animal.

As it may be figured, this creature is found in the Loch Ness, in the Scottish highlands.

Story/Context

Though possible sightings have been documented as far back as the 6th century, the cryptid first gained popularity in 1933, when George Spicer and Mrs Spicer (name unknown) encountered a 25 foot long creature with a long neck and no limbs crossing the road in front of their car, quickly disappearing into the loch. Public interest was piqued when a report was published in the Courier, leading to an influx of sightings and the official name of the Loch Ness Monster.
The first photograph of the beast was taken that same year by Hugh Gray, though it is suspected that it may depict his labrador or a swan or otter, rather than the mysterious cryptid. The most famous image, however, was taken by Robert Kenneth Wilson in 1934, which showcases the head and neck of Nessie.
